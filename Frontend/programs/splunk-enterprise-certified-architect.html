Program Overview

  • ▹ SPLK-3001 exam preparation and certification
  • ▹ Splunk deployment methodology and architecture planning
  • ▹ Data collection design and intake strategy
  • ▹ Distributed deployment sizing and capacity planning
  • ▹ Indexer clustering and search head clustering
  • ▹ Deployment troubleshooting and operational best practices

Who Should Attend

Splunk administrators, platform engineers, solution architects, and senior operations professionals designing or scaling distributed Splunk Enterprise deployments.

Prerequisites

Splunk Enterprise Certified Admin or equivalent production experience. Strong knowledge of distributed Splunk, Linux administration, networking, and storage planning is recommended.

What You Get

Architect-level design labs, sizing worksheets, reference deployment blueprints, full clustering exercises, mock exams, and expert review sessions.

Course Curriculum

Advanced 10-14 week training aligned to Splunk Enterprise Certified Architect exam objectives and real-world deployment design work.

Module 1-2: Deployment Methodology (Weeks 1-2)

Build the architectural foundation for successful Splunk Enterprise deployments.

Architecture Framework

Splunk deployment lifecycle · Requirements gathering · Use case mapping to design · Reference architectures

Deployment Models

Standalone, distributed, hybrid · Single-site vs multisite · On-prem, cloud, mixed · Availability tradeoffs

Planning Best Practices

Stakeholder workshops · Non-functional requirements · Operational readiness · Risk identification

Module 3-4: Data Collection & Sizing (Weeks 3-4)

Design data intake pipelines and size infrastructure correctly for enterprise scale.

Data Collection Strategy

Source inventory · Forwarder decision-making · HEC, syslog, scripted inputs · Source type standards

Capacity Planning

Daily ingest sizing · Storage forecasting · Compute/memory sizing · Growth modeling

Data Pipeline Design

Parsing, indexing, search separation · Load balancing · Data filtering · Collection resiliency

Module 5-6: Distributed Deployment Design (Weeks 5-6)

Architect standard distributed Splunk Enterprise environments for performance and resilience.

Distributed Search Design

Search head/indexer separation · Search affinity · Knowledge object deployment · Deployer strategies

Network & Infrastructure

Port requirements · Latency/bandwidth · Storage design · Virtualization/hardware tradeoffs

Security & Governance

TLS, certificates · RBAC in distributed setups · Auditability · Configuration governance

Module 7-8: Indexer Clustering (Weeks 7-8)

Plan, deploy, manage, and troubleshoot resilient indexer clustering architectures.

Cluster Planning

Replication/search factor · Single-site/multisite · Bucket replication · Manager sizing

Cluster Operations

Peer onboarding · Rolling restarts · Rebalancing · SmartStore in clusters

Troubleshooting Clusters

Health diagnostics · Peer loss recovery · Bucket issues · Replication lag

Module 9-10: Search Head Clustering (Weeks 9-10)

Design and troubleshoot search head clusters for scale, redundancy, and knowledge consistency.

SHC Architecture

Captain election · Deployer design · Knowledge bundle replication · Search artifact replication

Operational Best Practices

App packaging · Scheduled searches at scale · Search concurrency · Load balancer integration

SHC Troubleshooting

Captain changes · Rolling restarts · Member drift · Recovery and rebuild

Module 11-12: Architecture Troubleshooting & Exam Prep (Weeks 11-12)

Validate deployment designs, solve platform issues, and prepare for the architect certification exam.

Architecture Review Labs

Distributed design assessments · Sizing worksheet walkthroughs · Failure scenario exercises · Blueprint review

Practice Exams

Full-length architect mock exams · Scenario-based questions · Topic gap analysis · Detailed explanations

Final Review

Deployment methodology recap · Cluster management revision · Architect exam strategies · Expert Q&A

Hands-on Labs & Projects

Reference Architecture Lab

Create a complete Splunk Enterprise reference design for an enterprise use case, including topology, sizing, storage, network flow, and operational ownership.

Cluster Build Workshop

Frequently Asked Questions

How is the Architect certification different from Enterprise Admin?

Architect focuses on design, sizing, clustering, and methodology; Admin focuses on daily operations and maintenance.

Do I need clustering experience before starting?

Prior exposure to distributed Splunk is helpful, but clustering is taught from planning to troubleshooting.

Will this course cover multisite deployments?

Yes, multisite indexer clustering and multisite search head clustering are covered in depth.

Is sizing covered with practical examples?

Absolutely, you'll work through real-world sizing worksheets and capacity planning exercises.

What roles benefit most from this certification?

Splunk Architects, Senior Platform Engineers, and Solution Architects.

How difficult is the Architect exam?

It's advanced, but our design labs, mock exams, and troubleshooting simulations prepare you thoroughly.
